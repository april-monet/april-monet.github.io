Flourless Chocolate Cake

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 25-27 minutes
  • Yield: One 8-inch cake
  • Serves: 8 - 12

Recipe courtesy of King Arthur Flour

Ingredients

Cake:

  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup (8 Tbl.) unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 tsp. salt
    • * Espresso enhances chocolate's flavor as much as vanilla does. Using 1 tsp. will simply enhance the flavor, while 2 tsp. will lend a hint of mocha to the cake.
  • 1 - 2 tsp. instant espresso (such as Starbucks Via Italian Roast)*
  • 1 tsp. Vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened Dutch-process cocoa powder

Ganache Glaze:

  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream

Vanilla Mascarpone Whipped Cream (optional):

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 4 oz. Mascarpone
  • 1 tsp. Vanilla extract
  • Granulated sugar to taste (1 Tbl. or more)

Berry Sauce (optional):

  • 1/2 or all of a 16 oz. package of frozen mixed berries
  • Granulated sugar to taste (1/2 cup or more)
  • 1 cup of water

Method

Cake:
  1. Preheat oven to 375F. Lightly grease an 8-inch round cake pan; cut a piece of parchment to fit, lay it in the bottom of the pan, and grease it.
  2. Melt chocolate chips and butter in a double-boiler over medium-low heat, then transfer to a mixing bowl.
  3. Stir in the sugar, salt, instant espresso, and vanilla.
    4. ** An alternate way to mix the cocoa powder to prevent clumps is to add the liquids to the cocoa, a bit at a time, whisking into a paste that then becomes more liquid as the rest is added.
  4. Add the eggs, beating briefly until smooth. Add the cocoa powder, and mix just to combine.**
  5. Spoon the batter into the prepared pan.
  6. Bake the cake for 25 minutes; the top will have formed a thin crust, and it should reach an internal temperature of 200F.
  7. Remove cake from oven and set on a wire rack (in pan) to cool for 5 minutes.
  8. Loosen the edges of the cake from the pan and turn it out onto a serving plate. Allow to cool completely before glazing.
Glaze:
  1. To make the glaze, warm the cream in the double-boiler until hot but not simmering.
  2. Add the chocolate and stir until melted and combined smoothly with the cream.
  3. Pour the glaze over the cake starting at the center and then circling outward, until it drips down the sides slightly.
  4. Allow glaze to cool and set for several hours before serving. May be refrigerated for up to a week, covered, once glaze is cool.
Optional toppings:***
*** These are highly recommended for their complementary flavors, which lighten the intensity and richness of the cake.

Vanilla Mascarpone Whipped Cream:

  1. Chill mixing bowl and beaters in freezer until frosty.
  2. Combine heavy cream, Mascarpone, vanilla, and sugar in chilled bown and beat with stand mixer on medium until soft peaks form.
  3. Test for sweetness and add more sugar if desired. Continue beating on medium-high until stiff peaks form.
  4. Refrigerate until ready to use. Top individual slices of cake with a substantial spoonful or two, and drizzle with berry sauce.
Berry Sauce:
  1. Bring berries, water, and sugar to a simmer in a medium pot over medium heat.
  2. Simmer until berries have lost their shape and liquid has reduced to a thick syrup. Test periodically for level of sweetness, and add more sugar if desired.
  3. Citrus zest and/or spices can also be added to taste.
  4. Cool sauce in the refrigerator, unless serving immediately. Drizzle over cake and whipped cream topping.
