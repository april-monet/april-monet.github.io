Créme Brûlée

  • Prep: 15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 35 minutes
  • Yield: 6 servings in 6 oz. ramekins

Recipe attributed to: Amy & Jacky

Ingredients

Custard:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 6 Tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 7 large egg yolks
  • 1 pinch of salt

Caremelized sugar top:

  • 1 cup sugar or vanilla sugar

Special Equipment

  • 8-qt electric pressure cooker, such as Instant Pot Ultra
  • Porcelain ramekins (6 6 oz.)
  • Blender or food processor
  • Steam basket, bakeware sling, or trivet for lifting ramekins out of pressure cooker

Method

Custard:
  1. Separate egg yolks from whites, into a large mixing bowl.
  2. In a medium saucepan, warm cream, vanilla, and salt over medium heat. Stir frequently until hot but not boiling.
  3. Add sugar to the egg yolks and mix well with a whisk. Temper by pouring a small amount of the warmed cream mixture into the large mixing bowl. Mix well, then gradually add the rest of cream mixture.
  4. Strain through a wire mesh sieve/strainer into a large pitcher (such as a 4 cup Pyrex measuring cup).
  5. Pour the custard into 6 6oz. ramekins until 85% full. Tap ramekins on counter several times to remove air bubbles.
  6. Add 2 cups of water to 8-quart Instant Pot/electric pressure cooker, and place a steamer rack or bakeware sling inside. Place ramekins inside, stacking or cooking in two batches.
  7. Cook on Low Pressure for 13 minutes, and 15 minutes Natural Release. Release any remaining pressure with Quick Release.
  8. Check that the custard jiggles slightly in the middle. If the whole custard moves/is too liquid, continue cooking for another 3 - 5 minutes.
  9. Place ramekins on a cooling rack. Once cooled to room temperature, cover and refrigerate until ready to add topping. If serving immediately, leave at room temperature and proceed to make the topping.
Carmelized Sugar:
  1. Spread sugar in an even layer in a heavy-bottomed saucepan. Prepare a baking sheet with a piece of parchment and place nearby. If custard is chilled, remove the ramekins from the refrigerator and bring to room temperature.
  2. On medium heat, heat sugar until it melts. With a rubber spatula or silicone spoon, move the sugar gently as needed to incorporate unmelted sugar and to keep it from burning. Do not stir vigorously!
  3. Once melted, sugar will begin to brown quickly and can burn easily. When it is medium to dark brown in color (use your sense of smell to decide), remove from heat and pour the carmelized sugar onto parchment in a long, thick strip.
  4. Let the sugar cool until set, and you can the edge off easily.
  5. Break the caramelized sugar into pieces that fit in your blender. Pulse to blend to fine crumbs or powder.
  6. Sprinkle a layer of the sugar powder over the top of each custard. Place the ramekins on a baking sheet. Move rack to top of oven, and place tray with ramekins on it.
  7. Turn on broiler, and leave the the oven door open a crack so you can see the sugar melt. Once the sugar has melted and covers the top of the custard, remove it from the oven.
  8. Let ramekins cool slightly, until sugar has set again in a nice crackable layer atop the custard, then serve.
