Chocolate Dream (with Irish Cream) Cheesecake

Ingredients

Filling:

  • 16 oz. Cream Cheese
  • 8 oz. Mascarpone
  • 1/2 cup Sour Cream
  • 3 Large Eggs
  • 1 1/4 cups Sugar
  • 1 tsp. Vanilla Extract
  • 1 tsp. Bailey's or other brand Irish Cream Liqueur
  • Approximately 4 oz. dark chocolate (about half a standard bar)

Ganache Topping:

  • 4-8 oz. of dark chocolate
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream (check measurement)
  • 1/4 tsp. Irish Cream, if desired

Crust:

  • 2 cups Oreo cookie crumbs (about one package)
  • 4-5 Tblspn. Butter, melted

Other Equipment:

  • 8-quart electric pressure cooker
  • Hand mixer or stand mixer
  • Silicone bakeware sling
  • 8-inch cheesecake pan with removeable base
  • Blender or food processor
  • Silicone mitts or other oven mitts
  • Aluminum foil
  • Paper towels
  • Double boiler
  • Rubber spatula

Method

  1. Take cream cheese, Mascarpone, sour cream, and eggs out of refrigerator 2-4 hours in advance, and bring to room temperature.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 F, and line cheesecake pan with foil.
  3. Twist open Oreos and scrape off filling, then crush the cookies in blender or food processor to fine crumbs. Measure out 2 cups. Add melted butter and combine with hands or spoon until crumbs are moist and hold together slightly.
  4. Press the crumbs into the pan and about 1 inch up the sides, keeping as even a thickness as possible. Bake for 10-15 minutes, until you can smell the crust (toasty, but not burnt). Remove from oven and, once cool enough, place in freezer while making filling.
  5. Melt chocolate in double boiler over medium-low heat, stirring with rubber spatula. Once melted, remove pot from heat and set on a trivet to cool to room temperature (about 85 degrees Fahrenheit).
  6. Mix cream cheese, Mascarpone, sour cream, and sugar on low to medium speed, until smooth.
  7. Add vanilla and Irish Cream and mix to combine. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing on a very low speed to prevent bubbles from forming.
  8. Check that the melted chocolate is about the same temperature as the base mixture. A bit at a time, gradually add chocolate and mix at low speed or with a spoon, until concentration of chocolate reaches your desired level of flavoring.
  9. Remove crust from freezer, and pour filling into crust. Filling should come up over the edge of the side crust, to prevent it from getting soggy.
  10. Pour 2 cups of water into Instant Pot/pressure cooker, and add trivet. Set cheesecake pan on silicone sling, and lower onto trivet. Close and lock lid.
  11. Cook for 35 minutes on high pressure, and allow pot to do a natural release.
  12. Remove cheesecake using mitts, and set on trivet or wire rack to cool to room temperature. Use paper towels to soak up any water that has condensed on the surface of the cheesecake.
  13. Keep cheesecake in pan or remove to a plate (but keep foil on) and refrigerate overnight.
  14. Remove cheesecake from pan, and carefully remove foil from sides and base (use a large spatula to help). Set on a wire rack over a plate or rimmed pan.
  15. In double boiler, heat cream on medium-low heat. Chop or shave remaining chocolate into small pieces, then add to cream.
  16. Stir chocolate and cream with rubber spatula until melted and combined. If desired, add a 1/4 tsp. of Irish Cream.
  17. Remove pot from heat and set on trivet to cool to room temperature. Once cool (but not set), pour over cheesecake from center outward. Excess ganache will drip off sides onto pan or plate.
  18. Cool cheesecake in refrigerator again until chocolate is set, then slice and serve. Makes 8 large slices or 12 medium-small slices.
