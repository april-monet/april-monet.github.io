Ingredients

Filling:

  • 16 oz. Cream Cheese
  • 8 oz. Mascarpone
  • 1/2 cup Sour Cream
  • 3 Large Eggs
  • 1 1/4 cups Sugar
  • 1 tsp. Vanilla Extract

Crust:

  • 2 cups Graham Cracker crumbs (about one package)
  • 4-5 Tblspn. Butter, melted

Other Equipment:

  • 8-quart electric pressure cooker
  • Hand mixer or stand mixer
  • Silicone bakeware sling
  • 8-inch cheesecake pan with removeable base
  • Blender or food processor
  • Silicone mitts or other oven mitts
  • Aluminum foil
  • Paper towels

Method

  1. Take cream cheese, Mascarpone, sour cream, and eggs out of refrigerator 2-4 hours in advance, and bring to room temperature.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 F, and line cheesecake pan with foil.
  3. Crush Graham crackers in blender or food processor to fine crumbs. Add melted butter and combine with hands or spoon until crumbs are moist and hold together slightly.
  4. Press the crumbs into the pan and about 1 inch up the sides, keeping as even a thickness as possible. Bake for 10-15 minutes, until you can smell the crust (toasty, but not burnt). Remove from oven and, once cool enough, place in freezer while making filling.
  5. Mix cream cheese, Mascarpone, sour cream, and sugar on low to medium speed, until smooth.
  6. Add vanilla and mix to combine. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing on a very low speed to prevent bubbles from forming. At this point, other flavorings would be added.
  7. Remove crust from freezer, and pour filling into crust. Filling should come up over the edge of the side crust, to prevent it from getting soggy.
  8. Pour 2 cups of water into Instant Pot/pressure cooker, and add trivet. Set cheesecake pan on silicone sling, and lower onto trivet. Close and lock lid.
  9. Cook for 35 minutes on high pressure, and allow pot to do a natural release.
  10. Remove cheesecake using mitts, and set on trivet or wire rack to cool to room temperature. Use paper towels to soak up any water that has condensed on the surface of the cheesecake.
  11. Keep cheesecake in pan or remove to a plate (but keep foil on) and refrigerate overnight.
  12. Remove cheesecake from pan, and carefully remove foil from sides and base (use a large spatula to help). Add toppings if desired, then slice and serve. Makes 8 large slices or 12 medium-small slices.
